Unit Economics
WHY PLAYERS SPEND
The TDC Flywheel

Players invest in TDC → own valuable territory → earn ad revenue → reinvest in game → territory becomes more valuable → more players join. Each new player increases everyone's TDC value.

1. Organic land pressure
As player count grows, unclaimed land disappears. Early players capture prime real estate cheap. FOMO drives acquisition.
2. Loss aversion spending
Players who built valuable territories (earning ad revenue) spend to defend them. The more invested, the more they defend.
3. Alliance social pressure
Guilds of 25 players where 3 are heavy spenders creates pressure on others to contribute. Social commitment drives spending.
4. Real yield on TDC
Ad revenue creates a genuine yield on holding land. This is not P2W — it's land ownership. Completely different psychological model.

Risk Assessment
RISKS & MITIGATIONS
HIGH
Blockchain regulation (TDC utility token)
Mitigation: TDC structured as pure utility token (not security). Legal opinion secured pre-launch. MiCA compliance framework built-in. Withdrawal capped at €500/day per player. No investment solicitation language anywhere.
HIGH
Real-time sync at 100k+ concurrent players
Mitigation: Go game server (100k+ WS connections proven in load tests). Redis cluster as hot state, Postgres as recovery. Continental sharding — EU failure doesn't affect APAC. H3 spatial partitioning limits per-pod load.
MEDIUM
Bot / anti-cheat in clicker economy
Mitigation: Kafka event stream + ML behavioral analysis + device fingerprinting. Auto-ban pipeline tested before public launch. Click rate hard-capped. CAPTCHA-free (UX preserved) through fingerprinting + behavioral patterns.
MEDIUM
Geographically sensitive territories (Kashmir, Taiwan, Palestine)
Mitigation: Neutral OSM admin-level boundary data. Legal disclaimer layer at game launch. Content policy document by soft launch. In-game territories explicitly labeled "game fiction, not political statements."
LOW
P2W balance complaints destroying community
Mitigation: Hard-coded caps in smart contract (non-negotiable). Public transparency dashboard showing % F2P in top leaderboards. Quarterly balance patches with community voting. No pay-to-skip mechanics — only pay-to-enhance.
